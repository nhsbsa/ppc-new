Brief

Having to pay for a lot of NHS prescriptions seperately may become expensive. It may be cheaper to buy a prescription prepayment certificate (PPC) - effectively a prescription "season ticket".

A PPC covers all NHS prescriptions, including NHS dental prescriptions, no matter how many items are needed.

However, it does not cover the cost of other health-related items, such as the provision of wigs and fabric supports, which are only provided through the hospital service.

There are 3 PPC options to choose from:

  1. The 3-month PPC which costs £31.25 and will save money if a person needs more than 3 prescribed items in 3 months.
  2. The 12-month PPC which costs £111.60 and will save a person money if they need more than 11 prescribed items in a year.
  3. The hormone replacement therapy (HRT) PPC which costs £19.30 and will save a person money if they need more than 2 prescribed HRT items in a year.

The HRT PPC is only for HRT medicines licensed to treat menopause symptoms, but can be used regardless of the reasons why certain medicines are prescribed for someone. Find out more about the HRT PPC.

2. Certificate Duration page - Speech recognition issue

An issue has been discovered when using speech recognition (Voice Control on Mac) on this page. The user is offered three radio buttons, and two have the same label, saying 'Single payment'. Testers have attempted to say 'single payment' and the amount, but Voice Control times out before their sentence can be completed.

The agreed solution for this issue was quite simple: to change the labels on radio buttons to be different from one another.

3. Certificate Start Date page - Speech recognition issue

A similar issue has been reported on this page. In the previous version, there were two sets of date fields, meaning there are two Day, Month and Year fields. When using Voice Control on Mac, it was discovered that it was not possible to differentiate between the two fields, because naturally they are labelled the same way.

After considering several different options, the agree solution has been to remove both radio buttons, have only one set of date fields on the page, and present the information previously hidden within the two radio buttons as standalone content. Information about refunds has been moved into a Details component, for users who wish to backdate their certificate and have a valid FP57 form from that day.

Apple/Google Wallet certificate storage facilities and changes to content to better align with user needs.

Hypothesis A

We reviewed CSAT feedback and user needs, specifically in relation to:

  • Certificate storage facilities
  • Clearer info about prescriptions becoming free at age 60
  • Info around existing exemption/benefits which entitle users to free prescriptions
  • More information around refund rules and payment expectations
  • Clearer info around when a PPC will/will not autorenew

Certificate storage facilities:

From reviewing recent CSAT feedback, and general comments made about the PPC application, we identified that it would be in users' best interests to add both Google and Apple Wallet certificate storage facilities.

Allowing users to add their PPC to their digital wallet on their mobile devices, enables users to easily access their certificate without rummaging around their email inbox or digging around for their paper certificate which can be easily lost or damaged.


Clearer info about prescriptions becoming free at age 60:

We have received many complaints from users who have had a PPC that has failed to auto-renew because they are coming up to age 60, some have ended up with PCNs.

Whilst we are looking at reinstating the reminder for people coming up to 60, it would also be helpful if clearer information is provided within the application user journey so we are giving users every opportunity to make the right decision for them, particularly when selecting the length of their certificate.

This is because for users approaching age 60, they may find it more cost-effective to buy multiple 3-month PPCs if for example, they turn 60 within 6 months and therefore, wouldn't get the full use out of a 12-month certificate.

We have now provided clearer information regarding prescriptions becoming free at age 60 by adding new content when the system flags up a users who are aged 58 or 59.

View the Age 59 screen and/or view the Age 58 screen.

We have also provided content around approaching age 60 and certificate auto-renewals throughout the user journey. For example, you can see on the Certificate Duration screen that this information has been clearly highlighted to users through inset text.


Info around existing exemption/benefits which entitle users to free prescriptions:

Some users are unaware that some exemptions and benefits already entitle them to free NHS prescriptions, e.g., MATEX and Universal Credit.

In order to prevent users purchasing something they don't need i.e., a PPC in this instance, we now signpost users to check if they're already entitled to free NHS prescriptions by using our Eligibility Checker which has been added to the Start screen using an Action Link component.

The current Live DOB screen (as of 13/09/2023) says ‘We collect this information to check if you qualify for free prescriptions’. This is misleading to users, as we only check our own exemption database. We now clarify at this point that we ask this question because it helps us to identify if a person's age already entitles them to free prescriptions, e.g., over 60 or under 16, etc.

Again, as we cannot advise the user about all exemptions/benefits, signposting users to our Eligbility Checker via the Start screen will prevent users from purchasing a PPC which they may not need.


More information around refund rules and payment expectations:

Complaints about refunds/cancelled Direct Debits are really high and users frequently mention they had no idea that they couldn’t cancel their PPC or request a refund.

To help users understand more about the terms and conditions associated with PPCs, we have added more content to the Confirm and submit screen seen within the Direct Debit journey, and signposted users to the NHSBSA website to encourage users to read and agree to the PPC T&Cs prior to buying a PPC.


Clearer info around when a PPC will/will not autorenew:

A PPC paid by Direct Debit will not always automatically renew. The current user journey, particularly on the Certificate fulfilment screen is currently misleading about this. As it stands, the Live screen says, "Your certificate will automatically renew after 12 months, as long as you don't cancel your Direct Debit.", however, there are many reasons why a user's Direct Debit may not auto-renew. For example, if a user fails to keep up with monthly payments, the Direct Debit is being paid from someone else's account or, the user is approaching age 60. This list is not exhaustive.

We have now strengthed info regarding PPC renewals throughout the user journey regarding PPC renewals by including clearer content which you can view on the version 4 prototype Certificate fulfilment screen and when users are identified as being age 58/59 which are linked above.

Start page

  • More content has been added to the Start screen of the PPC user journey to better inform users as they consider purchasing a PPC.

    As a UCD team, we've considered the multiple routes that a broad range of users might hear about PPCs and how they might access the online service. Whilst some users may land directly on the NHSBSA website, other users may have been signposted from external websites such as GOV.UK or NHS.UK - there is a lot of content on the NHS's website around PPCs, detailing important information such as PPCs covering dental prescriptions and making it clear to the user that they can claim as many prescriptions as they need with a valid PPC. We felt that whilst some users may be aware of this, others may not - as various ways of purchasing a PPC exist. For instance, some users may hear of PPC at a pharmacy, others may have heard of the certificate through friends or relatives.

    We have therefore decided to update the info provided on the start page of the user journey to include more important information we felt was missing, such as info around dental prescriptions and the 'unlimited' amount of prescriptions any person can claim whilst holding a valid PPC.

    We also decided to include a 'Before you start', 'you'll need a valid debit or credit card in order to purchase a PPC' as felt that warning users about this prior to starting the journey as seen on other services such as HRT-PPC and MATEX Register, is relevant so that they have what's needed to complete the journey at hand.

Fulfilment and Confirmation screens

  • Content about plastic cards has now been removed from both the Fulfilment and Confirmation screens.

    Content about plastic cards no longer being sent out to users has now been removed from both the Fulfilment and Confirmation screens as we felt it was inappropriate to tell users something that we used to and no longer do.

Confirmation screens

  • New components have been used on all Confirmation screens.

    As tested across HRT-PPC, we know that the card-like components used to differentiate the confirmation panel (now seen in green) from the certificate details (now seen in blue) have had a really positive impact on users. Users are able to clearly recognise that they've successufully completed their application for a PPC and can also identify their certificate details easily as they're contained in a card-like component which as mentioned above, we used to send out. The new blue component really stands out amongst all of the other content and will make it easier for users to take a photo or screenshot of.
